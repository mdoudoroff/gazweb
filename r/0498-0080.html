Nail in the Coffin

45 ml Caol Ila 12 Years

22.5 ml Drambuie

22.5 ml Carpano Antica Formula

15 ml fresh lemon juice

7.5 ml honey syrup (2:1)

1 cinnamon stick, as garnish

1 orange twist, as garnish

Shake over ice and double-strain over large ice into an old-fashioned glass. Use a microplane grater to grate fresh cinnamon. Squeeze the twist over the drink, then wrap it around the cinnamon stick. Set next to the ice and drink up!

Adapted from a recipe by Tommy Klus, St. Jack, Portland, OR.

“This is the cocktail I entered in the Drambuie nail or fail for the competition in Portland. The Carpano sits nicely behind the complexity of both the Caol Ila and the Drambuie, adding to its balance. The cocktail did take 1st against some pretty fierce competition. Anyway, try it out, see what you think. I made if a few times over the past week and really enjoyed it. Hope you like it.” Tommy Klus.

gaz sez: I loves me some Caol Ila, I do, so this was a natural pick for me. Tommy is correct when he point out that the Carpano plays nice with the scotch and the Drambuie here. It makes its presence known, but it doesn’t take over the drink by any means. For the heck of it I made this one sans lemon juice and honey, and it worked well that way, too.
