Prieuré de Sion

40 ml Darroze 10 Year

15 ml St. Germain

5 ml Suze

8 dashes Peychaud’s bitters

1 lemon twist

Stir over ice and strain into a chilled (preferably frozen) elegant coupette or small goblet. Squeeze the twist over the drink, then discard.

Adapted from a recipe by Isak Cornelis von Werther, The Gold Bar, Nobis Hotel, Stockholm.

“I wanted to make a drink that really used the qualities of an otherwise often forgotten spirit. The liqueur and the Suze provide the initial taste balancing sweet fruitiness and bitter gentian notes. Secondary is the earthy, and oaky flavours of the armagnac and the Peychaud’s providing a slight spike to an otherwise very smooth cocktail. Lemon nose to lift it up from the otherwise quite heavy flavour profile.”

Later

Gaz: Why the name? I know Prieuré de Sion from the book Holy Blood, Holy Grail. Presume that’s where you got it?

Isak: The name does indeed hail from that very Priory of Zion, but I did not remember that I read about it in the DaVinci Code when I choose the name.

It was a failed attempt at naming the drink to “The Holy Grail” in French, where my friend was not quite as fluent in said language as she first thought. The correct term Graal or Saint Graal did not feel as striking as the original attempt, so Prieuré de Sion just kinda stuck.” Isak Cornelis von Werther.

gaz sez: Finally I get a great recipe with an armagnac base. I love armagnac, and it’s seldom utilized for cocktailian pursuits. For me it’s the Suze, plus the excessive amount of Peychaud’s bitters, that bring this drink to life. They make the St Germain behave itself, and pay high compliments to the armagnac.
