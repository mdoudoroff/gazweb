P.C.C.G. Punch

1 L green tea

5 oranges

266 g (1.66 cups) demerara sugar

1 l Rhum JM Blanc 50º

75 cl Laird’s applejack

12 cl Averna amaro

12 cl St. Elizabeth Allspice Dram

24 cl Averna Limon de Sicilia

20 dashes Peychaud’s bitters

48 cl cold water

First, make green tea, heating water in the microwave for 2 minutes and adding 3 Lipton green tea bags to the water and setting aside.

While the tea bags steep, you must make what is called “oleo saccharum” by peeling the skin off the five oranges, making sure not to get any pith. Peel gently to just take off the top layer of the orange peel. This will help the oils from the peel escape. Place peels in a large nonreactive bowl and add the sugar. Muddle the sugar and the orange peels with a flat or rounded end muddler (do not us a barbed muddler, you do not want to break up the orange peel, you just want to press the oil out of the skin to saturate the sugar). Set your sugar and peels to the side and let stand for 15 minutes to release more oil into the sugar.

Take your tea bags out of the tea and set the tea aside. Juice the oranges, making sure to get every drop out of them. Strain your juice through a tea strainer to keep out the pulp, and you may refrigerate the juice if you like. After fifteen minutes has passed, stir the orange peels and sugar one last time, then add the green tea. Stir until all the sugar has dissolved. Add the orange juice and cold water. Strain this mixture through a sieve, separating the orange peels from the sluice. To this mix you may now add your rhum, applejack, Amaro, Allspice Dram, Limoni, and bitters. Let stand for 1 hour so the flavors may integrate. Pour into a punch glasses filled with ice and enjoy.

Adapted from a recipe by Sean D. Enright, Spoon, Pittsburgh, PA.

“Named for Pittsburgh Craft Cocktail Guild, which was a small group of like-livered members who eventually went on to create Pittsburgh’s chapter of the USBG.” Sean Enright.
