Where There’s Smoke There’s Sapphire

7.5 ml Ilegal mezcal reposado

1 cucumber slice (¼-inch)

a few papalo leaves or (6-8) cilantro sprigs

15 ml agave nectar

15 ml fresh lime juice

52.5 ml Bombay Sapphire gin

2 thin jalapeño slices (leave seeds in)

1 cucumber wheel, as garnish

1 cilantro sprig, as garnish

Rinse a rocks glass with the mezcal; add ice and set aside. Muddle the cucumber, papalo or cilantro, agave, and lime juice in a mixing glass. Add ice and the remaining ingredients. Shake and double-strain into the glass. Add the garnishes.

For a more potent smoky, spicy version, add the mezcal with the gin instead of using it to rinse the glass.

Adapted from a recipe by Anthony DeSerio, Tacuba/Swill, Branford, CT.

“Trying for two for two here :) This one is inspired by: 1. my first trip to Tales of the Cocktail, where cucumber and jalapeño were in a few of the wonderful cocktail tasting rooms, and notice the Sazerac rinse. 2. Working alongside (literally my bar is attached to the line of the open kitchen) an amazing Mexican chef who has been teaching me about new ingredients and flavor combinations, tequilas mezcals, rums, piscos... you get the idea. We were discussing the fact that mezcal is misunderstood up here in the Northeast. I mentioned gin is as well, though it is trying and making strides. That’s when I thought of gin and mezcal together… could it be done? I have hopes that I did on this one. Cheers!” Anthony DeSerio.
