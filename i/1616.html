raw sugar syrup

Also known as muscovado sugar syrup, demerara sugar syrup, turbinado sugar syrup, demerara simple syrup

Raw sugar syrup is generic for a simple syrup made with so-called raw sugar, which is not raw, but is less processed than refined sugar. Raw sugar—also known as Demerara sugar or muscovado sugar—retains a little more molasses flavor than refined sugar, and tends to have a larger grain. Consequently, simple syrups made with raw sugar can turn out slightly weaker than simple syrups made with refined sugar, at least when measuring the sugar by volume.

To make, simply combine one part raw sugar and one part water (by weight) in a sauce pan, heat and stir until dissolved. Cool and bottle.
