grenadine

Grenadine is a red or purple syrup sweetener and colorant made from pomegranate juice and/or artificial flavorings. (The word "grenadine" is French diminutive for “pomegranate”.) Usually non-alcoholic.

Grenadine is supposed to do three things in a cocktail: dye it crimson, sweeten it (more or less, depending on quantity), and contribute pomegranate flavor (again, more or less, depending on quantity).

Quite a few commercial grenadines are available, but they tend to either be artificially-colored sugar syrup that lacks pomegranate flavor or they’re weak naturally-flavored syrups that fail to correctly color the drink they're added to.

You can make your own by combining 2 cups POM pomegranate juice with 1 cup superfine sugar, 2 tbsp orange flower water and 1 oz vodka (as preservative). Shake in a sealed vessel until completely mixed and the sugar is dissolved. The resulting grenadine will excel in terms of sweetening and flavor. If the coloring effect is still too weak, try mixing in a little natural red food coloring.
